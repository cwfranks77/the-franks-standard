# Owner Reporting Guide — The Franks Standard

Backend reference for law-enforcement and fraud reporting. Owner tools read this file automatically.

---

## 1. Reporting fraud (general marketplace fraud)

1. Document the order ID, buyer/seller usernames, payment date, and screenshots of listings or messages.
2. Freeze the account from the owner safety console if active harm is ongoing.
3. Open or link a fraud case in the platform fraud engine.
4. File with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3): https://www.ic3.gov/
5. For payment-card fraud, also notify Stripe via the platform dashboard dispute/fraud tools.
6. Retain COA chain-of-custody records and authenticity reports for the case file.

---

## 2. Reporting threats (violence, extortion, harassment)

1. Preserve all messages, timestamps, and user IDs. Do not delete content.
2. Apply messaging freeze or full account freeze immediately.
3. Export activity logs and violation scanner matches for the user.
4. Contact local police for imminent danger (911 in the United States).
5. File a federal tip with FBI IC3 when threats cross state lines: https://www.ic3.gov/
6. Prepare a law-enforcement report draft from the owner console (do not auto-send).

---

## 3. Reporting counterfeits

1. Collect listing photos, seller statements, and any authenticity reports filed by buyers.
2. Remove or hold the listing; issue buyer refund if a verified counterfeit sale occurred.
3. Document serial numbers, COA documents, and third-party grading claims.
4. Report to the U.S. Secret Service field office nearest the seller or shipment destination: https://www.secretservice.gov/contact/field-offices
5. For imported goods, note U.S. Customs and Border Protection referral may apply.
6. Submit industry fraud alert draft (PII-redacted) from owner tools when appropriate.

---

## 4. Reporting stolen goods

1. Obtain police report number from the victim or reporting agency when available.
2. Match listing images and serials against reported stolen property databases when possible.
3. Freeze seller account and preserve shipping/return addresses from paid orders.
4. File with local law enforcement where the theft occurred (see local lookup below).
5. File supplemental report with FBI IC3 for interstate sales: https://www.ic3.gov/
6. Cooperate with subpoenas; use prepared law-enforcement export package.

---

## 5. Reporting child-related content (CSAM / exploitation)

**If a child is in immediate danger, call 911 (U.S.) or local emergency services first.**

1. Do not download, forward, or re-host the material.
2. Remove access: ban account, freeze store, preserve logs only (no content replication).
3. Report immediately to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline: https://report.cybertip.org/
4. Contact FBI IC3: https://www.ic3.gov/
5. Contact local police: https://www.usa.gov/local-governments
6. Document internal case ID and time of report for owner records only.
