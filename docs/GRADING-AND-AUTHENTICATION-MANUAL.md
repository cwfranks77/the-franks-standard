## Section 1 – The purpose of grading

Grading does two main things:

1. Describes the condition of a coin.
2. Helps determine its market value.

**Authentication** answers: “Is it real?”  
**Grading** answers: “How nice is it?”

For The Franks Standard:

- **AUTHENTICITY** first.
- **ACCURATE GRADING** second.
- **CLEAR, HONEST DESCRIPTIONS** always.

---

## Section 2 – The Sheldon scale (0–70)

The Sheldon scale runs from 1 to 70. In practice, you’ll mostly use:

| Range | Grades |
|-------|--------|
| PO–AG | 1–3 |
| G–VG | 4–10 |
| F–VF | 12–35 |
| XF | 40–45 |
| AU | 50–58 |
| MS | 60–70 |

**Basic descriptions:** PO-1 barely identifiable · AG-3 outlines visible · G-4/G-6 heavy wear · VG-8/10 more detail · F-12/15 moderate wear · VF-20–35 light–moderate wear · XF-40/45 light wear on high points · AU-50–58 slight wear, strong luster · MS-60–70 no wear, contact marks and strike differ.

---

## Section 3 – How wear progresses

Wear starts on **high points** (cheek, hair, eagle breast, wreath tips). As grade drops: high points flatten, fine lines disappear, luster fades from high points then fields.

See full manual in `.txt` for silver, copper, and nickel wear patterns.

---

## Section 4 – Strike vs wear

**Wear:** smooth rounded areas, luster broken on high points, circulation pattern.  
**Weak strike:** always weak on that date/mint; luster may remain; no rounded wear. On Morgans, weak eagle breast can be strike in MS — check luster.

---

## Section 5 – Luster, cleaning, and damage

Disclose cleaning, polishing, scratches, rim dings, holes, bends. Use **details grades** (e.g. “XF Details – Cleaned”).

---

## Section 6 – Step-by-step grading workflow

1. Authenticate first — do not grade counterfeits.  
2. Identify type, date, mint.  
3. Check damage and cleaning.  
4. Evaluate wear.  
5. Evaluate luster.  
6. Strike and eye appeal.  
7. Assign grade + notes.
