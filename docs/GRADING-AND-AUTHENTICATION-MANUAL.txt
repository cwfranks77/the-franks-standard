SECTION 1 – THE PURPOSE OF GRADING
==================================================

Grading does two main things:

1) Describes the condition of a coin.
2) Helps determine its market value.

Authentication answers: “Is it real?”
Grading answers: “How nice is it?”

For your platform (The Franks Standard), you want:
- AUTHENTICITY first.
- ACCURATE GRADING second.
- CLEAR, HONEST DESCRIPTIONS always.

==================================================
SECTION 2 – THE SHELDON SCALE (0–70)
==================================================

The Sheldon scale runs from 1 to 70.
In practice, you’ll mostly use these ranges:

- PO–AG (Poor to About Good): 1–3
- G–VG (Good to Very Good): 4–10
- F–VF (Fine to Very Fine): 12–35
- XF (Extremely Fine): 40–45
- AU (About Uncirculated): 50–58
- MS (Mint State): 60–70

BASIC DESCRIPTIONS:

PO-1: Barely identifiable, almost no detail.
AG-3: Outlines visible, rims merge with lettering.
G-4/G-6: Major details visible, heavy wear, rims mostly complete.
VG-8/VG-10: More detail, but still heavily worn.
F-12/F-15: Moderate wear, major details clear.
VF-20 to VF-35: Light to moderate wear, many finer details visible.
XF-40/XF-45: Light wear on high points only.
AU-50 to AU-58: Very slight wear, mostly full detail, strong luster.
MS-60 to MS-70: No wear, only contact marks and strike quality differ.

SECTION 3 – HOW WEAR PROGRESSES ON COINS
==================================================

3.1 GENERAL WEAR PATTERN

Wear starts on the highest points of the design:
- On portraits: cheek, hair above ear, eyebrow, cap.
- On eagles: breast, legs, top of wings.
- On wreaths: highest leaves and berries.

As grade goes down:
- High points flatten.
- Fine lines disappear.
- Luster fades from high points, then from fields.

3.2 SILVER COINS (E.G., MORGANS, PEACE DOLLARS)

HIGH POINTS:
- Obverse: Liberty’s cheek, hair above ear, cap folds.
- Reverse: Eagle’s breast, top of wings, leg feathers.

WEAR PATTERN:
- XF: Slight flattening on cheek and eagle’s breast.
- VF: More flattening, but major lines still visible.
- F: Many fine details gone, but main shapes clear.
- G: Major design still visible, but flat.

3.3 COPPER COINS (E.G., INDIAN HEAD, LINCOLN CENTS)

HIGH POINTS:
- Obverse: hair, cheek, jawline.
- Reverse: wheat lines (wheat cents), shield details, etc.

WEAR PATTERN:
- XF: Slight wear on hair and cheek.
- VF: Hair details merging, cheek flatter.
- F: Hair mostly flat, outlines strong.
- G: Design visible but flat and worn.

3.4 NICKEL COINS (E.G., BUFFALO, JEFFERSON)

HIGH POINTS:
- Buffalo: hip, shoulder, head.
- Portraits: cheek, hair, jawline.

Nickel is harder and wears differently:
- Often shows “smooth” wear.
- Details fade but surfaces can still look decent.

SECTION 4 – STRIKE VS WEAR (CRITICAL CONCEPT)
==================================================

Not all weak detail is wear.
Some coins were weakly struck from the start.

WEAR:
- Smooth, rounded areas.
- Luster breaks on high points.
- Consistent with circulation.

WEAK STRIKE:
- Certain areas always weak on that type/date.
- Luster may still be present in “weak” areas.
- No smooth, rounded wear pattern.

EXAMPLE (MORGAN DOLLARS):
- Some dates/mints have weak eagle breast feathers even in Mint State.
- Look at luster: if it’s still there, it’s likely strike, not wear.

SECTION 5 – LUSTER, CLEANING, AND DAMAGE
==================================================

5.1 LUSTER

Luster is the way light reflects off the microscopic flow lines created during striking.

REAL, ORIGINAL LUSTER:
- Cartwheel effect on silver.
- Even, smooth, and directional.

LOST LUSTER:
- From circulation wear.
- High points dull first.
- Eventually, whole coin looks flat.

5.2 CLEANING

Improper cleaning is one of the biggest problems in grading.

SIGNS OF CLEANING:
- Hairline scratches in one direction.
- Unnatural brightness.
- “Wiped” look.
- Dull, lifeless surfaces after harsh cleaning.

LIGHT CLEANING:
- May still be market-acceptable in lower grades.
- Still must be disclosed honestly.

HARSH CLEANING:
- Deep scratches.
- Polished look.
- Often considered “details grade” (e.g., “XF Details – Cleaned”).

5.3 POLISHING

Polished coins:
- Look shiny but wrong.
- Surfaces are smooth and reflective like chrome.
- Luster is destroyed.
- Often have rounded details.

These should be described as:
- “Polished”
- “Improperly cleaned”
- “Not original surfaces”

5.4 DAMAGE

Common types:
- Scratches.
- Rim dings.
- Holes.
- Bends.
- Gouges.

Grading with damage:
- You can still assign an approximate grade (e.g., VF).
- But you must note: “VF details – scratched” or similar.

SECTION 6 – STEP-BY-STEP GRADING WORKFLOW
==================================================

This is the process you can follow for every coin you grade.

6.1 STEP 1 – AUTHENTICATE FIRST

Before grading:
- Confirm the coin is genuine.
- Use your authentication framework:
  - Weight, diameter, thickness.
  - Magnet test.
  - Visual diagnostics.
  - Known fake patterns.

If it fails authentication:
- DO NOT GRADE.
- Mark as “Not Genuine” or “Counterfeit”.

6.2 STEP 2 – IDENTIFY TYPE, DATE, MINT MARK

Record:
- Country.
- Denomination.
- Type (e.g., Morgan Dollar).
- Date.
- Mint mark.

This matters because:
- Some dates are weakly struck.
- Some dates are more valuable.
- Some dates are heavily counterfeited.

6.3 STEP 3 – CHECK FOR DAMAGE AND CLEANING

Before assigning a numeric grade:
- Look for:
  - Scratches.
  - Holes.
  - Bends.
  - Harsh cleaning.
  - Polishing.

If present:
- Plan to use a “details” description:
  - Example: “VF Details – Cleaned”
  - Example: “XF Details – Scratched”

6.4 STEP 4 – EVALUATE WEAR

Use a reference (or your own experience) to:
- Identify the high points.
- See how much detail is left.
- Decide which range it fits:
  - G, VG, F, VF, XF, AU, MS.

6.5 STEP 5 – EVALUATE LUSTER

For higher grades (XF and up):
- Check how much luster remains.
- XF: luster mostly in protected areas.
- AU: most luster present, slight wear on high points.
- MS: full luster, no wear, only contact marks.

6.6 STEP 6 – EVALUATE STRIKE AND EYE APPEAL

STRIKE:
- Strong, average, or weak?
- Weak strike can lower eye appeal but not necessarily grade.

EYE APPEAL:
- Toning: attractive or ugly?
- Spots, stains, or discoloration?
- Overall look: pleasing or not?

6.7 STEP 7 – ASSIGN GRADE + NOTES

Final output should include:
- Numeric grade (or range).
- Any “details” notes (cleaned, scratched, etc.).
- Short description of eye appeal.

EXAMPLES:
- “VF-30 – Original surfaces, light even wear.”
- “AU-55 – Strong luster, slight rub on cheek and eagle’s breast.”
- “XF Details – Cleaned, hairlines visible in fields.”

SECTION 7 – GRADING MORGAN DOLLARS SPECIFICALLY
==================================================

7.1 KEY HIGH POINTS

OBVERSE:
- Cheek.
- Hair above ear.
- Cap folds.

REVERSE:
- Eagle’s breast.
- Top of wings.
- Leg feathers.

7.2 GRADE RANGES (MORGAN EXAMPLES)

G-4:
- Liberty’s outline visible.
- Date and lettering readable.
- Major design flat.

F-12:
- More hair detail visible.
- Some lines in cap and wreath.
- Eagle’s outline clear, some feather detail.

VF-20 to VF-30:
- Hair detail above ear visible.
- More detail in cap and wreath.
- Eagle’s breast still worn but some feather separation.

XF-40/XF-45:
- Light wear on cheek and eagle’s breast.
- Many fine details still present.
- Some luster in protected areas.

AU-50 to AU-58:
- Very slight wear on cheek and eagle’s breast.
- Strong luster overall.
- High points show just a bit of rub.

MS-60 to MS-65:
- No wear.
- Full luster.
- Grade depends on number and severity of contact marks and eye appeal.

SECTION 8 – HOW TO DESCRIBE COINS FOR YOUR PLATFORM
==================================================

For The Franks Standard, your descriptions should be:

1) Honest.
2) Clear.
3) Consistent.

8.1 BASIC DESCRIPTION TEMPLATE

- Type: (e.g., Morgan Silver Dollar)
- Date/Mint: (e.g., 1885-CC)
- Grade: (e.g., VF-30, AU-55, or “VF Details – Cleaned”)
- Authentication: (e.g., “Authenticated by The Franks Standard”)
- Notes:
  - Wear pattern.
  - Luster.
  - Any damage or cleaning.
  - Eye appeal.

EXAMPLE:
“1885-CC Morgan Dollar – VF-30. Original surfaces with even gray toning. Moderate wear on cheek and eagle’s breast, but major details clear. No harsh cleaning or major damage observed. Authenticated and graded by The Franks Standard.”

8.2 DETAILS GRADES ON YOUR PLATFORM

When a coin has problems:
- Still describe the approximate grade.
- But clearly state the issue.

EXAMPLES:
- “XF Details – Cleaned, hairlines in fields.”
- “VF Details – Scratched, obverse field.”
- “AU Details – Rim ding at 3 o’clock.”

This keeps your marketplace honest and protects buyers.
