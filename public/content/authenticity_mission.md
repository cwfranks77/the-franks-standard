# Authenticity Mission Statement

## Zero tolerance for counterfeit goods

The Franks Standard maintains **zero tolerance** for knowingly counterfeit, misrepresented, or stolen merchandise. Sellers who attempt to pass fakes forfeit platform access. Buyers who file good-faith authenticity reports are protected through dispute and refund workflows.

## COA chain-of-custody

Every Certificate of Authenticity issued through TFS is logged in a **chain-of-custody registry**:

1. **Upload** — seller or grader submits evidence and item photos.
2. **Issue** — platform assigns a serial (e.g. `FS-2026-000001`) and binds it to the listing fingerprint.
3. **Transfer** — ownership changes are recorded when items sell and COAs are re-issued where applicable.
4. **Verify** — buyers and third parties can validate serials through the public COA lookup tools.

This creates an auditable trail — not a PDF that anyone can Photoshop.

## Seller accountability

Sellers digitally sign current marketplace policies before listing. Integrity holds, account freezes, and platform debt tracking apply when refunds are issued for seller-at-fault reasons (counterfeit, not as described, dispute upheld). Excellence programs reward compliant sellers; enforcement programs remove repeat offenders.

## Buyer protection

Buyers receive:

- Escrow-style payment flow through Stripe
- Structured dispute opening with evidence upload
- Authenticity reporting on suspicious listings
- Mandatory contact verification before high-risk actions

## Enforcement systems overview

| Layer | What it does |
|-------|----------------|
| Proof gate | Blocks publish without COA or signed guarantee |
| Violation scanner | Scans messages, reviews, disputes, and reports |
| Fraud case engine | Opens cases for severe violations |
| Account freeze / ban | Stops buying, selling, and messaging |
| Anti-rejoin | Device, browser, and IP fingerprint checks at registration |
| Law enforcement prep | Owner-reviewed report packages (never auto-sent) |

Authenticity is not marketing copy on TFS. It is architecture.
