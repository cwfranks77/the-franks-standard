# Marketplace Safety Overview

The Franks Standard runs multiple backend safety systems in parallel. This page explains what protects the community — without requiring buyers or sellers to become security experts.

## Violation scanner

Automated pattern scanning runs on messages, reviews, dispute text, contact forms, and authenticity reports. Categories include threats, harassment, hate speech, illegal activity, counterfeit promotion, fraud schemes, and off-platform payment solicitation.

**Outcomes:** log only (minor), 72-hour messaging freeze (minor repeat), full account freeze (major), fraud case + ban path (severe).

## Fraud detection

The fraud case engine opens structured cases when severe violations occur or when ops/AI triage flags high-risk disputes. Evidence is appended over time. Industry fraud alerts and law-enforcement report drafts can be prepared — **owner review required before any external send**.

## Dispute resolution

Buyers open disputes on verified orders. Sellers are notified. Cases can escalate to TFS review. Resolved disputes trigger support follow-up emails so we can measure resolution quality.

## Account freeze system

Accounts may be frozen for policy violations, platform debt (seller-at-fault refunds), or integrity holds. Frozen accounts cannot list, buy, message, or checkout until cleared.

## Permanent ban system

Permanent bans apply to the account and can extend to device fingerprints, browser fingerprints, and IP addresses associated with fraud or repeat abuse.

## Anti-rejoin system

Registration fingerprints are stored and checked against banned identifiers. Users attempting to return after a ban are blocked at signup safety check.

## Device and IP fingerprinting

Device and browser fingerprints (client-provided hashes) plus IP addresses are logged with activity events and checked during signup, checkout, and enforcement actions. VPN/datacenter traffic may require phone verification.

## Law enforcement cooperation

Owner tools include a reporting guide with links to FBI IC3, U.S. Secret Service field offices, NCMEC CyberTipline, and local agency lookup. Evidence packages are prepared in-platform; the owner decides what to submit externally.

---

Safety on TFS is continuous — not a checkbox at signup.
