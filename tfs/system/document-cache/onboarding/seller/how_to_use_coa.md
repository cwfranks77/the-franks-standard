# How to Use COA (Certificate of Authenticity)

COA is The Franks Standard's **chain-of-custody** system for high-trust listings. It links evidence, listings, and orders so buyers and the platform can trace authenticity claims.

---

## What COA Is

- Uploaded proof: certificates, dealer invoices, manufacturer docs, or category-approved evidence
- **Hashed and logged** server-side when you upload
- Tied to your **listing** and, after sale, to the **order**
- **Serialized** certificates receive a unique platform serial number at issue

COA is not optional where category policy marks it required.

---

## When You Need COA

Check your category during listing creation. If you see **COA required**:

- You must upload before or during listing (some flows allow post-list upload before first sale—follow on-screen prompts)
- Listings without required COA may be suppressed or removed

Categories without COA may still benefit from voluntary documentation for buyer confidence.

---

## How to Upload

1. In the listing flow, open **COA / Authenticity**
2. Upload clear images or PDF (readable text, no heavy filters)
3. Enter **serial numbers** for serialized items exactly as on the item
4. Submit — you'll see confirmation when the file is stored and hashed

Keep originals; buyers may ask questions pre-sale.

---

## Serialized COA Numbers

When the platform issues a COA serial:

- It is **unique** to that certificate instance
- It appears on the listing/order record where verification is enabled
- Reusing a serial across listings triggers fraud review

---

## After the Sale

COA records stay linked to the order for dispute and authenticity investigations. Do not alter or replace files after sale without platform process.

If you discover you uploaded the wrong document **before** shipment, contact support immediately with the order ID.

---

## Buyer Verification

Where verification tools are enabled, buyers can check COA serials against platform records. Ensure what you upload matches what you ship.

---

## What COA Does Not Do

- It does not replace honest condition descriptions
- It does not guarantee subjective grades (e.g. "mint" opinions)
- It does not protect off-platform transactions—checkout must stay on TFS

---

## Violations

| Action | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Fake or altered certificate | Fraud case, suspension |
| Reused serial | Listing removal, account review |
| Missing required COA | Listing suppressed |
| Stock COA image from web | Authenticity violation |

---

## Tips for Strong COA Listings

- Photograph the physical item **and** the certificate together when possible
- Include dealer name, date, and model on the document
- For competition audio (B&C lane), power ratings and model numbers must match the unit
