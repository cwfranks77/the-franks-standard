# How Disputes Work (Sellers)

Disputes are the formal process when a buyer believes an order went wrong. Treat them seriously—your response and evidence matter.

---

## When a Buyer Opens a Dispute

You'll be notified when a buyer opens a dispute from **My Orders**. Common reasons:

- Item not received (INR)
- Not as described (condition, wrong item, missing parts)
- Authenticity concern

---

## Your Response Window

Sellers have a **limited time** to respond (exact duration shown in the dispute UI and policy). In your response:

1. Acknowledge the issue calmly
2. Provide tracking, photos, messages, and listing snapshots
3. Propose a fair resolution when appropriate (replacement part, partial refund request through policy—not off-platform)

Missing the window may result in default escalation toward the buyer.

---

## Evidence That Helps

| Situation | Useful evidence |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Delivered | Carrier tracking showing delivery scan |
| Condition dispute | Your listing photos, pre-ship photos, packing photos |
| Authenticity | COA upload record, serial photos |
| Buyer damage | Document if credible and time-stamped |

---

## Escalation

If buyer and seller cannot agree, the case may **escalate to platform review**. Owner or designated review considers policy and evidence. AI support cannot override outcomes.

Outcomes may favor buyer or seller, including:

- Full or partial refund
- Denial of claim
- Return instructions per policy

Refunds process through **Stripe** when approved.

---

## Authenticity Disputes

If the buyer claims counterfeit or misrepresentation:

- COA and serial records are reviewed
- Repeat seller patterns matter
- Severe findings may become **fraud cases** separate from the single dispute

---

## What Not to Do

- Don't harass the buyer in messages
- Don't offer off-platform refund to make it "go away"
- Don't ship a replacement without a documented agreement through platform flows

---

## Impact on Your Account

Unresolved disputes and patterns of INAD claims affect seller metrics and may trigger listing limits or review. Honest documentation is your defense.
