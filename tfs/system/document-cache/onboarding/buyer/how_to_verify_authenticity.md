# How to Verify Authenticity (Buyers)

High-trust gear deserves more than a seller's word. Use these steps before and after purchase on The Franks Standard.

---

## Before You Buy

### 1. Read the Listing Carefully

- Model number, year, and condition match what you expect
- Photos show labels, serial plates, and wear—not only glamour shots
- Description states what's included and any modifications

### 2. Check COA Indicators

On categories that support **Certificate of Authenticity (COA)**:

- Look for COA badge or section on the listing
- Review uploaded certificate images in the listing flow
- Note whether a **platform serial** is displayed where issued

COA is **chain-of-custody**: files are hashed, logged, and linked to the listing (and your order after purchase).

### 3. Verify Serials (Where Enabled)

If platform verification is enabled for the listing:

- Use the **verify COA serial** tool with the serial shown
- A match supports that the certificate is on file for that item
- No COA on a non-required category does not automatically mean fake—use judgment and seller history

### 4. Review Seller History

- Star average and review count
- Other listings—consistent photos and descriptions?
- Account age and response patterns in messages

### 5. Ask Questions On-Platform

Request:

- Photo of serial next to today's date (if high value)
- Photo of certificate next to the item
- Proof of authorized dealer purchase when claimed

Keep answers in **messages** for dispute evidence if needed.

---

## Red Flags

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| "DM for better price off app" | Do not buy; report |
| Stock photos only on used item | Ask for real photos or skip |
| Price far below market | Extra scrutiny |
| Refusal to show serial | High risk |
| Pressure to pay quickly off-platform | Report |

---

## At Checkout

- Pay **only** through TFS checkout
- Confirm shipping address—your protection ties to the order record

---

## After Delivery

1. Inspect immediately against listing photos
2. Photograph serial and condition upon receipt
3. If misrepresented, **open dispute** from My Orders with evidence
4. Use **Report authenticity** for counterfeit suspicion

---

## Authenticity Disputes

If you believe an item is fake or materially misrepresented:

- Open dispute with comparison photos and expert notes if available
- Reference COA serial if applicable
- Do not publicly harass the seller—platform review handles escalation

---

## B&C Competition Audio

Mislabeled RMS, wrong coil configuration, and refurb-as-new are common issues. Cross-check specs against manufacturer data; use COA and serial photos aggressively for high-dollar amps and subs.
