# Messaging Pillars — The Franks Standard

Five pillars anchor all campaigns, onboarding, and support narratives. Every major message should map to at least one pillar.

---

## Pillar 1 — Authentic Gear, Verifiable Trust

**Idea:** High-trust categories need more than a title and stock photo.

**Proof points**
- COA chain-of-custody: uploads hashed, logged, linked to listings and orders
- Serialized certificates with unique numbers where issued
- Category policies requiring evidence for eligible listings
- Buyer verification tools where enabled

**Sample lines**
- "Authenticity you can trace—not just trust."
- "COA-backed when the category demands it."

**Never say**
- "Every item is authenticated" (unless category + COA explicitly apply)

---

## Pillar 2 — Fraud Protection & Safe Commerce

**Idea:** The platform is the payment rail and the safety net.

**Proof points**
- Checkout only on TFS; off-platform pay is a reportable violation
- Monitoring: counterfeit claims, messaging patterns, device/IP signals
- Fraud cases escalate to owner review
- Law enforcement packages prepared manually when appropriate

**Sample lines**
- "Pay on-platform. Always."
- "Report scams—we investigate; you stay safe."

**Never say**
- "Impossible to get scammed"
- "We'll arrest them" (unless referring to documented LE cooperation process)

---

## Pillar 3 — Fair Marketplace Operations

**Idea:** Honest sellers deserve clear fees, payouts, and dispute windows.

**Proof points**
- Default 10% platform fee; plan and B&C qualification may vary
- Stripe-connected payouts on schedule
- Disputes: buyer opens, seller responds, escalation path
- Reviews and seller history visible to buyers

**Sample lines**
- "Structured payouts. Clear dispute process."
- "Compete on quality, not on who avoids platform fees."

---

## Pillar 4 — Louisiana-Compliant Tax & Checkout

**Idea:** Tax follows the shipment, not guesswork.

**Proof points**
- Sales tax calculated from **shipping destination ZIP code**
- Marketplace facilitator alignment (Louisiana Act 22 posture)
- Checkout displays tax before payment confirmation
- 25% owner business income tax reserve handled in backend financial logic (internal—not buyer-facing marketing detail unless required)

**Sample lines**
- "Tax calculated from where it ships—automatically at checkout."
- "Louisiana marketplace rules built into every order."

**Buyer-facing focus:** shipping ZIP, transparent checkout total.

---

## Pillar 5 — Owner-Operated Accountability

**Idea:** A human stands behind escalation—not endless deflection.

**Proof points**
- Owner tools: fraud review, dispute review, lockdown, audit export
- AI assists FAQs; cannot override owner rulings
- Platform status and logs for financial, security, and violation activity

**Sample lines**
- "Owner-operated marketplace—real review when it matters."
- "Escalations reach a decision-maker."

---

## Pillar Map — B&C Performance Audio (Marketing)

B&C inherits pillars 1–5 but emphasizes:

| Emphasis | Message |
|----------|---------|
| Niche catalog | Competition audio, SPL, build-oriented inventory |
| Seller economics | Reduced fees for qualified B&C sellers |
| Brand lane | Dark-theme marketing identity; shared TFS rails |
| Community | Show-floor credibility meets platform checkout |

B&C messaging **references** these pillars; it does not replace or weaken them.

---

## Anti-Messages (What We Don't Claim)

- We are not the cheapest marketplace on the internet.
- We are not anonymous—seller and buyer accounts tie to real transactions.
- We are not a single-brand store—we are multi-vendor.
- B&C is not a separate unregulated site—it is a lane inside TFS.
