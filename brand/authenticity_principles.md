# Authenticity Principles — The Franks Standard

Operational and cultural rules for how authenticity is defined, enforced, and communicated on the platform.

---

## Definition

On TFS, **authentic** means the item matches its listing: correct item, condition, provenance, and—where required—documented chain-of-custody via COA. Authenticity is not a vibe; it is evidenced behavior by sellers and verifiable artifacts where policies apply.

---

## Principles

### 1. Evidence Over Claims

Listings must show what buyers will receive: multiple angles, serial numbers when present, damage disclosed, model numbers accurate. COA categories require uploads that are hashed and retained in chain-of-custody logs.

### 2. Category Policy Is the Floor

Each category may require COA, photos of labels/serials, or prohibit certain item types. Sellers accept category rules at listing time; "I didn't know" is not a defense for repeat violations.

### 3. Serial Integrity

When COA issues a serialized certificate, the serial is unique and tied to the listing/order lifecycle. Duplication or reuse triggers fraud review.

### 4. No Bait-and-Switch

Substituting a different model, grade, or replica after sale is a material misrepresentation—grounds for dispute and account action.

### 5. Provenance Honesty

"Sourced from estate," "authorized dealer," or "NOS" must be supportable. Inflated provenance is treated as authenticity fraud.

### 6. Buyer Verification Where Enabled

Buyers may verify COA serials through platform tools when enabled. Verification supports trust; absence of COA on non-required categories does not automatically mean counterfeit.

### 7. Reports Are Taken Seriously

**Report authenticity** flows feed monitoring and may open fraud cases. False reports waste resources and may violate policy.

### 8. Education, Then Enforcement

First-time fixable errors may receive warnings and listing corrections. Pattern abuse, counterfeits, or off-platform circumvention escalate to freezes, bans, and owner review.

---

## Seller Responsibilities

- Upload required COA and photos before or during listing as policy dictates
- Respond to authenticity questions in messages honestly
- Honor dispute evidence requests promptly
- Never ask buyers to complete sales off-platform to "avoid fees"

---

## Buyer Responsibilities

- Read listings and COA badges carefully
- Use platform messaging for documentation
- Open disputes with photos and order details—not public call-outs that escalate risk
- Report suspected counterfeits with specifics (order ID, listing link, screenshots)

---

## Platform Responsibilities

- Maintain COA hashing, logging, and order linkage
- Operate fraud monitoring and owner escalation
- Apply consistent dispute standards based on evidence
- Never auto-send law enforcement reports without manual owner process

---

## B&C Performance Audio Lane

Competition audio is a frequent target for mislabeled power handling, rebranded gear, and used-as-new listings. B&C sellers are held to the **same authenticity principles** as Franks sellers. Marketing may stress performance credibility; enforcement is identical.

---

## What We Don't Do

- We don't pre-authenticate every SKU in a warehouse (multi-vendor model).
- We don't guarantee subjective grading opinions—condition must match declared grade.
- AI support cannot certify an item authentic from a chat description alone.
