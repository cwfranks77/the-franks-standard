# Tone of Voice — The Franks Standard

Guidelines for all customer-facing copy: site strings, emails, AI agents, support macros, and approved marketing.

---

## Core Personality

We sound like a **skilled shop owner who has seen scams and still believes in fair deals**—not a corporation hiding behind passive voice, and not a hype account pushing "insane steals."

- **Plain:** Short sentences. Active verbs. Define terms once (COA, dispute, payout).
- **Honest:** We state what we do and don't do. AI and support cannot promise refund amounts or timelines we don't control.
- **Steady:** Especially in fraud and dispute messages—calm beats alarmist.
- **Inclusive:** Assume the reader is smart but may not know marketplace jargon.

---

## Pronouns & Address

| Audience | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Buyers | "You" — focus on safety and clarity |
| Sellers | "You" — focus on compliance and earnings |
| Platform | "We" — The Franks Standard / TFS |
| Owner escalation | "Our team" or "owner review" — avoid fake omniscience |

---

## Word Choices

**Prefer**

- authentic, verified, on-platform, checkout, shipping ZIP, certificate, chain-of-custody
- dispute, evidence, payout, policy, report
- owner-operated, marketplace

**Use carefully**

- guarantee (only where legally accurate)
- instant (payouts and refunds have schedules)
- free (clarify what's free vs fee-inclusive)

**Avoid**

- bro, fam, crushing it (except quoted user content)
- "100% safe" — use "built-in protection" instead
- instructing buyers to confront scammers
- mixing Franks and B&C in the same sentence unless explaining ecosystem

---

## Tone by Channel

### Product UI
Minimal, scannable. Lead with the action ("Open dispute," "Connect Stripe"). Secondary line explains why.

### Email
Subject lines: specific ("Your payout is processing") not clickbait. Body: one primary CTA.

### AI chat / phone agents
Empathetic, bounded. Escalate when asked for legal outcomes, law enforcement, or policy overrides.

### Marketing (including B&C)
Energy allowed; facts required. Mention B&C's dark theme and lower fees as **lane attributes**, not UI instructions in Franks files.

### Legal / policy
Still readable. Headings, bullets, examples. No sarcasm.

## Examples

**Instead of:**  
"Per our policy, transactions conducted extra-platform violate TOS."

**Write:**  
"Pay only through The Franks Standard. Off-platform payment requests violate seller policy and aren't protected."

---

**Instead of:**  
"Tax will be computed pursuant to applicable law."

**Write:**  
"Sales tax is calculated from your shipping ZIP at checkout, including Louisiana marketplace rules."

---

**Instead of:**  
"Your dispute will definitely be refunded."

**Write:**  
"We'll review your dispute and evidence. Outcomes follow platform policy; refunds process through Stripe when approved."

---

## Accessibility

- Don't rely on color words alone ("red badge")—pair with label text.
- Expand acronyms on first use in long-form content.
- Reading level: aim for grade 8–10 in buyer/seller guides; technical docs may go higher.

---

## Review Checklist

Before publishing copy:

1. Is it true today (fees, tax, features)?
2. Does it keep payments on-platform?
3. Does it avoid blending B&C and Franks in product contexts?
4. Would a non-technical reader know what to do next?
