# How Payouts Work (Sellers)

Understanding when and how you get paid helps you plan cash flow and avoid surprises.

---

## Overview

1. Buyer pays through **TFS checkout** (Stripe)
2. Platform **fee** is deducted (default 10%; your plan or B&C qualification may differ)
3. **Sales tax** is collected from buyer based on **shipping ZIP**—remitted per marketplace facilitator process (not your ad-hoc calculation)
4. **Net proceeds** move toward your **Stripe Connect** account on the payout schedule

---

## Connecting Stripe

Before your first sale:

1. Go to **Seller Settings → Payments**
2. Complete Stripe Connect onboarding
3. Verify bank account and identity as Stripe requires

Payouts cannot send until Connect is complete and in good standing.

---

## Fee Structure

| Component | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Platform fee | Deducted at sale; see dashboard for your rate |
| Payment processing | Handled within Stripe flows |
| B&C qualification | Eligible competition-audio sellers may see reduced platform fee |

Featured and starter plans may have different fee tables—confirm in seller dashboard, not third-party blogs.

---

## Payout Schedule

Payout timing depends on:

- Stripe Connect schedule (e.g. rolling delays for new sellers)
- Order status (shipped/delivered policies)
- **Open disputes or fraud holds**

Funds may be **held** while a dispute is active or fraud review is pending.

---

## Holds & Freezes

Your payout may pause if:

- A buyer opened a dispute on the order
- Fraud review flagged the transaction or account
- Owner placed a financial freeze for policy investigation

You'll see status in **Seller Dashboard → Payouts**. Contact support with order IDs if you believe a hold is in error—do not ask buyers to pay again off-platform.

---

## Refunds

Approved refunds from disputes or seller-initiated flows process through **Stripe** back to the buyer. Refunded amounts adjust your net payout; chargebacks follow Stripe and platform policy.

---

## Tax Reporting

You are responsible for your income tax obligations as a seller. The platform operates Louisiana marketplace facilitator tax on **buyer sales tax** at checkout—distinct from your business income. Consult a tax professional for 1099/K and state obligations.

Internal owner systems may reserve business income tax per operator policy—this does not change your Stripe Connect onboarding requirements.

---

## Wholesale / Distributor Transfers

Where your store is a fulfillment node for distributor inventory, instant wholesale transfers may apply per backend configuration—see your seller agreement and dashboard line items.

---

## Best Practices

- Keep Connect bank details current
- Ship with tracking to reduce INR disputes
- Download payout reports monthly for records
- Never accept "direct deposit" from buyers outside Stripe
