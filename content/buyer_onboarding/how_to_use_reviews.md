# How to Use Reviews

Reviews help honest sellers stand out and warn buyers about repeat problems. Use them fairly and read them critically.

---

## Reading Reviews

When viewing a listing or store:

- **Average rating** — quick signal, not the whole story
- **Review count** — low count means less data; read each review
- **Recency** — old reviews may not reflect current seller behavior
- **Themes** — shipping speed, accuracy, packaging, communication

Look for specifics: "serial matched COA" beats "great seller!!!"

---

## What Reviews Cover Well

- Item matched description
- Shipping time and packaging
- Seller responsiveness
- Would buy again (subjective but useful)

---

## What Reviews Don't Replace

- COA verification tools
- Your own inspection of listing photos
- Dispute process for a specific order gone wrong

A single bad review may be a misunderstanding; a **pattern** of INAD claims is a warning.

---

## Leaving a Review

After delivery, leave an honest review:

1. Rate the experience (follow on-screen scale)
2. Comment on **condition vs listing**, shipping, and communication
3. Avoid personal attacks, slurs, or unrelated drama
4. Do not mention off-platform payment attempts in ways that dox you—report those separately

Reviews are public and tied to your order history.

---

## Changing or Disputing Reviews

Platform policy may allow editing within a window or response from sellers—check current help for timelines. Review retaliation (seller harassing you for a bad review) is a violation—report it.

---

## Fake Reviews

If you suspect shill reviews:

- Note identical phrasing across accounts
- Report to support with listing/store links

We monitor manipulation patterns; owner review handles severe cases.

---

## B&C Lane Sellers

Competition audio buyers often know each other from shows—reviews still must reflect **the transaction on TFS**, not unrelated reputation from events.
