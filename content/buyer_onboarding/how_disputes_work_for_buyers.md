# How Disputes Work (Buyers)

If an order goes wrong, open a dispute from your account—don't chase the seller off-platform.

---

## When to Open a Dispute

Valid reasons include:

- **Item not received** — no delivery or carrier shows no progress
- **Not as described** — wrong item, worse condition, missing parts
- **Authenticity concern** — counterfeit or material misrepresentation

Open promptly after you discover the issue. Waiting weeks without contact weakens your case.

---

## How to Open a Dispute

1. Go to **My Orders**
2. Select the order
3. Choose **Open dispute**
4. Pick the reason category
5. Describe the problem clearly
6. **Attach evidence** — photos, screenshots of messages, tracking

---

## What Happens Next

1. **Seller is notified** and has a limited window to respond
2. Seller may offer tracking, explanation, or resolution
3. If unresolved, case **escalates to platform review**
4. Outcome may favor buyer or seller per policy
5. Approved **refunds** process through Stripe

AI support cannot promise refund amounts or exact timelines.

---

## Strong Buyer Evidence

| Issue | Attach |
|-------|--------|
| Damage / wrong item | Photos of item, labels, packing |
| Not received | Tracking screenshot; carrier inquiry if needed |
| Authenticity | Comparison to listing, COA mismatch, expert note if available |
| Missing parts | Photo of everything received vs listing |

---

## Returns

If policy requires return for refund:

- Follow return instructions in the dispute thread
- Use trackable shipping
- Keep photos of return packaging

---

## Off-Platform Pressure

If the seller asks you to cancel the dispute and pay/refund off-platform—**decline and report**. You lose protection outside checkout.

---

## Outcomes You May See

- Full refund
- Partial refund
- No refund (claim denied)
- Replacement per seller agreement through platform

Owner rulings on escalated cases are final per published policy.

---

## Authenticity & Fraud

Severe counterfeit cases may spawn a separate **fraud case** for owner review. You do not need to confront the seller in person or online.
